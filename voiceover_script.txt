We have all gotten this email.

An investor. Warm tone. Three paragraphs of enthusiasm. Then a pass buried so deep in compliments you have to read it twice to confirm they said no.

This is Subtext. A Chrome extension that reads corporate language and tells you what it actually means.

[BEAT 1 - INVESTOR EMAIL]

Here is the email from Marcus. He is, quote, really rooting for you.

Subtext gives it an 84 out of 100 on the BS score.

The archetype: The Eternal Encourager. Someone who has never said a hard thing directly in their professional life.

The translation of really rooting for you: goodbye.

The reply you actually want to send: Hi Marcus. Quick question. What does excited about the space mean in a world where you just passed? Asking for my runway.

[BEAT 2 - DOORDASH JOB POSTING]

Now the job posting. DoorDash. Work hard and play hard. No job is beneath you. In-office snacks.

Survival probability: twenty-two percent.

No job is beneath you translates to: we will ask you to do things that are beneath you, constantly, and then act surprised when you quit.

Translation of health insurance employee only, dependents not covered: the cold brew in the fridge is what we offer instead of maternity coverage.

The honest rewrite: this role will regularly require more than you agreed to. We have framed this as culture. It is not culture. It is a staffing model.

[BEAT 3 - HONEYCOMB JOB POSTING]

And Honeycomb. A passionate group of bees. Obsessed with craft. Collaborative and intense.

Survival probability: thirty-one percent.

Passionate group of bees translates to: you will work with people who believe that intensity is a personality trait.

The honest rewrite: we move fast, we care deeply, and we have not quite figured out what healthy boundaries look like. You will be expected to share our values, which are mostly about working more.

It is not mean. It is just accurate. That is the product.
