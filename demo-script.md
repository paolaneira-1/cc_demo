# Subtext — Demo Script

## Target audience: a room of women founders

These are people who have:
- Received at least one "excited about the space" email that was a pass
- Applied for or posted a job that said "competitive salary"
- Sat in a performance review that said "executive presence" and had no idea what that meant
- Scrolled through LinkedIn and felt a feeling they couldn't name (cringe + jealousy + contempt + exhaustion)

The comedy lands when the audience sees their own experience named precisely. The translation isn't the joke — the accuracy is the joke.

---

## Demo sequence (recommended order for maximum effect)

### Beat 1 — Open with the investor email (30 seconds)

**Use: `ie-001` — "The Classic Pass Disguised as Enthusiasm"**

Why first: Everyone in the room has received this exact email or knows someone who has. The opening establishes the premise immediately — this tool sees through the performance. The translation of "really rooting for you" → "goodbye" will get the first real laugh.

**Expected moment:** Show the email, then reveal the bs_score (target: 82), then scroll through the translations one by one. Pause on "love to stay in touch as things evolve" → "never contact me again unless you're raising at a higher valuation." That one lands.

**The archetype reveal:** "The Eternal Encourager" — read the description out loud. Room recognizes it.

**Finish with `the_reply_you_want`:** "Hi Marcus, thanks for the warm pass. Quick question — what does 'excited about the space' mean in a world where you just passed? Asking for my runway."

The room will laugh and immediately want to send it.

---

### Beat 2 — The performance review (45 seconds)

**Use: `pr-001` — "The Promotion-Proof Non-Feedback"**

Why second: The investor email is about external gatekeeping. The performance review is about internal gatekeeping. The one-two punch shows the tool works in every room women are evaluated in.

**Expected moment:** The translation of "executive presence" → "we don't know why we're not promoting you but it's definitely something about you" is the loudest moment in the demo. It names a thing that has happened to nearly every woman in a professional environment who has never received a satisfying definition of what executive presence means in practice.

**Show the honest_rewrite:** "We're not promoting you this cycle. We don't have a specific reason we can put in writing. You're good at your job. Work on being more visible to people who matter." Watch the room go quiet and then laugh.

**Finish with `the_reply_you_want`:** The question about what executive presence looks like in practice. Every woman in the room has wanted to ask this. The fact that they can generate it instantly is the product moment.

---

### Beat 3 — The job posting (45 seconds)

**Use: `jp-003` — "The Culture Fit Warning Sign"**

Why third: The "work hard and play hard" posting is the funniest because it is so blatant about what it is while genuinely believing it isn't. The survival probability (target: 24%) is the comedic centerpiece of this beat.

**Expected moment:** The survival_probability reveal. Land on it. "24% — you will burn out. The question is when." Let that sit for two seconds.

**Key translations to show:**
- "We work hard and play hard" → "the playing hard is mandatory and the working hard is non-optional"
- "Not a 9-to-5" → "we will regard your personal boundaries as a performance issue"
- "Health insurance (employee only, dependents not covered)" → just let this speak for itself, or translate as "the cold brew in the fridge is what we offer instead of maternity coverage"

**Finish with `the_reply_you_want`:** "Love the role — just confirming, when you say 'not a 9-to-5,' do you mean the hours are flexible or do you mean specifically that they exceed 40? Asking because I have a life."

---

### Beat 4 — LinkedIn (30 seconds, closer)

**Use: `lp-003` — "The Unsolicited Mentor"**

Why last: LinkedIn is the lightest beat — no one's livelihood depends on it. It's pure comedy and it closes the demo on a high, breathable note. Every person in the room has seen this exact post and had complicated feelings about it.

**Expected moment:** The cringe_score reveal. Target 7/10. Read the description: "High cringe. You feel something in your sternum."

**Key translation:** "I had coffee with a 22-year-old founder yesterday" → "I am the main character of this story and she exists to receive my wisdom."

**The honest_rewrite:** "I gave someone advice at a coffee meeting. They seemed to find it useful. Felt good. Recommend coffee meetings."

**The_reply_you_want:** "She said she needed to hear that. Did she though. Did she need to hear 'stop networking, start building relationships.' Was that the thing she needed."

Room laughs. Demo ends.

## What makes it work for women founders specifically

1. **The performance review beat is the anchor.** The "executive presence" translation names a specific, gendered experience that most female professionals have had and never seen clearly articulated. When it appears on screen, translated, with no hedging, it creates a moment of recognition that is simultaneously funny and genuinely cathartic.

2. **The investor email uses a female founder name.** The fixture uses "Sarah" receiving the email from "Marcus." This is not incidental — the power dynamic (male investor, female founder, warm language, hard pass) is familiar in a way that doesn't need to be stated.

3. **The `the_reply_you_want` format is the product's soul.** Women, specifically, have spent enormous cognitive energy managing how they come across in professional communications. The "reply you want to send but won't" externalizes that suppressed voice and gives it a body. That's not just funny — it's relieving.

4. **The survival probability number is darkly funny in the right way.** 24% implies that the person reading this job posting is already doing the math. That math is familiar to anyone who has taken a startup job and wondered if the company would still exist at their one-year anniversary.

5. **The LinkedIn beat should go last** because it's the only one that doesn't sting. It's pure play. It lets the audience exhale.

## Alternate sequence for shorter demos (90 seconds)

1. `ie-001` investor email — 40s — show full result
2. `pr-001` performance review — 35s — just the translations + the_reply_you_want
3. `lp-003` LinkedIn — 15s — just the one_liner and cringe_score

Skip the job posting if pressed for time. The investor and performance review beats carry the most emotional weight.
