Ever wanted to filter out the corporate BS?

The performance review that said "reset moment" and meant you are going on a PIP.
The investor who said "market timing" and meant pass.
The job posting that said "fast-paced environment" and meant understaffed and chaotic.

Introducing Subtext. A Chrome extension that reads between the lines.

[BEAT 1 - PERFORMANCE REVIEW EMAIL]

This is a manager feedback email. Warm opener. Then a 60-day alignment plan.

Subtext gives it a BS score and breaks it down phrase by phrase.

"This is a reset moment for both of us." Translation: you are going on a performance
improvement plan and we both know it.

The honest rewrite cuts it to one sentence. And yes — it writes the reply you actually
wanted to send.

[BEAT 2 - INVESTOR PASS EMAIL]

Now an investor rejection. Every VC pass follows the same structure. Compliment the team.
Blame the portfolio. End with "stay in touch."

Subtext reads the archetype instantly.

"The metrics aren't quite where we need them to be." Translation: we do not believe
in this yet.

"Would love to reconnect when you hit your milestones." Translation: please do not
reach out again.

[BEAT 3 - DOORDASH JOB POSTING]

DoorDash. No job is beneath you. Work hard and play hard.

Survival probability: twenty-two percent.

No job is beneath you means: we will ask you to do things that are beneath you,
constantly, and act surprised when you quit.

Health insurance, employee only, dependents not covered. The cold brew in the fridge
is what they offer instead.

[REAL WORLD USE CASES]

Here is why this matters.

Twenty-eight percent of US workers are actively looking for a new job right now. More
than a quarter of all job postings contain at least one red-flag phrase -- up from
eighteen percent just eight years ago. Every one of those people is trying to decode
what the posting actually means.

The average founder pitches fifty-eight investors to close one seed round. VCs fund
about one percent of what they see. Almost none of the rejections explain why. Founders
spend weeks interpreting emails instead of improving their pitch.

Ninety-five percent of managers say they are frustrated with their own review systems.
Half of all feedback is not actionable. Only fourteen percent say performance reviews
actually motivate anyone to improve.

Subtext works anywhere there is text. Gmail, job boards, LinkedIn, Slack. Any page,
any text, one click.
