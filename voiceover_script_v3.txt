Ever wanted to filter out the corporate BS?

The performance review that said "reset moment" and meant you are going on a PIP.
The investor who said "market timing" and meant pass.
The job posting that said "fast-paced environment" and meant understaffed and chaotic.

Introducing Subtext. A Chrome extension that reads between the lines.

[BEAT 1 - PERFORMANCE REVIEW EMAIL]

This is a manager feedback email. Warm opener. Then a 60-day alignment plan.

Subtext scores it 78 percent and tells you what it actually says.

"I'd like for us to treat this as a reset moment for both of us." Subtext headline:
Paola, you're on a PIP. They just renamed it to save your feelings.

And here is the honest rewrite. No hedging. No alignment plan. Just what this email
actually means.

[BEAT 2 - INVESTOR PASS EMAIL]

Now an investor rejection. Pass Ventures, passing after a first call.

Every VC pass follows the same structure. Compliment the team. Blame the portfolio.
End with "stay in touch."

You can write it before you read it. Subtext can too. It names the archetype the
moment it reads the subject line.

72 percent. The headline: we're passing, but want you to keep us warm forever.

And this line from the decoded section: "You seem smart, but smart doesn't write
checks."
